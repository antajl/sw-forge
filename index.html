Guide

Getting started

Data flow: Export JSON from SWEX and load it directly. SWOP CSV is not required.

Stat IDs: The exporter uses numeric stat identifiers (for example SPD is 8, CRate is 9). The app maps them to names everywhere — mains, substats, filters, and role logic.

Efficiency in the file: If the JSON includes an efficiency value per rune, the Rune Table and summaries use it; otherwise efficiency is computed in the browser.

Database slots: Your first SWEX file opens as Data 1. In App Settings you can store up to four exports, see wizard name/level from the JSON, and Swap the active profile.

Dashboard

Account progression: Combined score and the three depth metrics always use your full export. Under each metric you’ll see its point contribution toward the 100-point scale (e.g. +12 / 35 pts). By default the block is collapsed to one line (title, suggested stage, Apply, preset); use the chevron to expand for explanations and depth cards.

Global filters: Min level and Min grade apply to verdict cards, all charts, the efficiency histogram (including the median line), and Rune Table — not to the progression block above.

Verdict mix & cards: The stacked bar and the number tiles show the same counts; percentages are share of the current filtered total. Click a segment, a card, or a role row to jump to Rune Table with matching filters. If the dashboard min grade is Legend only, the table’s grade filter is preset to Legend; for Rare+ / Hero+ the table opens without a grade filter (the grid uses an exact grade pick, not a floor).

Copy summary: Separate control to the right of the Global filter card. Copies a text snapshot: preset, suggested stage, depth metrics, filters, verdict counts, role / set / slot charts, efficiency median and histogram buckets.

Charts: Role Distribution — count and average efficiency per role (custom roles appear after you save rules). Set Distribution — top sets by count. Slot Distribution — hover a row for the full main-stat breakdown. Efficiency Distribution — bucketed eff with median marker.

Account progression (Depth v2)

The block at the top of the Dashboard estimates how deep your rune inventory is — not your arena rank or monster box. It uses absolute counts and a quality ceiling so small accounts cannot look the same as long-term farms.

Independent of presets: The Combined score and the three metric cards ignore your Early / Mid / Late preset and Global Min Lvl. They always use the full Rare+ rune list from the loaded SWEX (rank ≥3). Changing the preset still changes verdicts everywhere else (summary cards, table, charts).

The three metrics

  • Speed Depth (35%) — Runes whose substat SPD total (roll + grind) is at least 18.
  • Power Depth (35%) — 6★ runes at exactly +15.
  • Elite Quality (30%) — Mean uncapped efficiency (can exceed 100%) on the top 50 runes, or on all runes if you have fewer than 50.

Combined score formula

  • Speed: min(Speed Depth ÷ 250, 1) × 35 — full points at 250+ qualifying runes.
  • Power: min(+15 count ÷ 600, 1) × 35 — full points at 600+ qualifying +15 six-stars.
  • Elite: min(max(0, eliteAvg − 80) ÷ 30, 1) × 30 — full points from 110% average upward (80 + 30).

Suggested stage labels

  • Early — score below 45
  • Mid — 45 up to (but not including) 85
  • Late — 85 or higher

Apply suggestion sets Your preset to the suggested stage and recalculates the app; you can override manually.

Efficiency note: SWEX has no eff field — the app computes it. The Rune Table uses capped 0–100%; Depth v2 uses uncapped values for the elite term.

Rune Table

Rune Table: Search, header filters for verdict, role, grade, set, slot, and main stat; sort columns; inspect innate and substats. Default sort Eff% descending. Use Export CSV for spreadsheets.

Eff% column: Prefers the exporter’s efficiency when present; otherwise shows the app’s calculated value (capped 0–100% in the grid; Depth v2 still uses uncapped math for its own score).

Target column: Shown when the verdict filter is Gem or Grind — for Grind, numeric goals (rounded up); for Gem, innate Replace from → to style hints plus other notes where relevant.

Rune Summary panel: Right-hand summary — counts and average efficiency by verdict and role for the rows you have filtered.

Debug: Parsed runes keep _raw from SWEX for troubleshooting in devtools.

Rune Rules tab

Sections: Engine — Constants (eight stats) and read-only God / Duo / stage-line previews. Verdict — three separate cards for Gem, Grind, and Reapp. Roles — archetype formulas and filters (full width). The last-opened section is remembered for this browser session.

Engine vs roles vs verdicts

Roles decide whether a rune matches an archetype (using formulas). Engine constants define God, Duo, Early/Mid/Late High Roll lines used for anchors and quality checks. Verdict cards layer Gem / Grind / Reapp on top of that pipeline.

Base substats for matching: Role assignment, Duo/High checks, and verdict thresholds use base rolled values (before gem or grind). Enchant and grind flags on each sub row are still used by the Gem and Grind rules themselves.

Formulas: Min Stats vs Require High Roll

Min Stats counts how many Include substats are present on the rune; stats listed as must-have are excluded from that count. Require High Roll (anchor) is a separate requirement — turning it on does not change the Min Stats number.

Grind verdict

Grind targets SPD / HP% / DEF% / ATK% only, comparing the rune to the Late × grade High Roll line from Engine (God is not a Grind target). The rune must be ungrinded and not enchanted on the target line; the gap multiplier tightens how far below the line is still acceptable (default 0.5 after preset migration v9 if you previously had the older default).

Verdict safety

A failing Gem quality gate does not force Sell if the rune already earned a role. Runes with no role and no Duo/High signal cannot end as Keep, Finish, Gem, or Grind — only Sell, Reapp, or Upgrade remain possible there.

Save: Edits in the Roles formula grids persist and reprocess runes as soon as you change a control. Engine constants and Verdict inputs (Gem / Grind gap / Reapp) are applied when you click Save & Recalculate at the bottom — use that after changing those sections so Dashboard and Rune Table stay in sync.
